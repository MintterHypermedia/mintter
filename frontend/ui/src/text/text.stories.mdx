# Text

The `Text` component is one of our most basic primitives, use it and combine it with other components to build more
complex UI patterns.

## Import

```tsx
import {Text} from '@mintter/ui/text'
```

## Usage

```jsx
<Text>This is my Text!</Text>
```

### Size

While you can leverage and combine the theme tokens to create any text style you want, we have some pre-built
combinations that are used across the UI, to avoid repeating the pattern on each implementation.

```jsx
<Text size="1">(1) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text size="2">(2) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text size="3">(3) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text size="4">(4) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text size="5">(5) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text size="6">(6) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text size="7">(7) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text size="8">(8) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text size="9">(9) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
```

> The default `Text` size is `3`.

### Alternative

When passing the `alt` prop to the `Text` component, the alternative font will be used.

```jsx
<Text alt size="1">(1) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text alt size="2">(2) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text alt size="3">(3) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text alt size="4">(4) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text alt size="5">(5) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text alt size="6">(6) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text alt size="7">(7) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text alt size="8">(8) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text alt size="9">(9) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
```

### Color

Just as with the variants; while you can virtually attach any color from the Theme to a `Text` component via the `css`
prop, we took all the colors that start with `$text-` and added a shorthand prop for calling them. For instance, if
there is a color named `$text-contrast`, the `color` prop you need to pass to the `Text` component is `contrast`.

```jsx
<Text color="default">The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text color="alt">The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text color="muted">The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text color="opposite" css={{backgroundColor: '$background-opposite'}}>The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text color="contrast">The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text color="primary">The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text color="secondary">The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text color="terciary">The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text color="success">The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text color="warning">The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
<Text color="danger">The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
```

> The default `Text` color is `default`
