# Text

The `Text` component is one of our most basic primitives, use it and combine it
with other components to build more complex UI patterns.

## Usage

```tsx
import {Text} from '@mintter/ui/text'
```

<Canvas isExpanded>
  <Text>This is my Text!</Text>
</Canvas>

### Text Variant

While you can leverage and combine the theme tokens to create any text style you
want, we have some pre-built combinations that are used across the UI, to avoid
repeating the pattern on each implementation.

<!-- prettier-ignore-start -->

<Canvas isColumn>
  <Text size="9">(huge) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text size="8">(h1) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text size="7">(h2) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text size="6">(h3) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text size="5">(h4) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text size="4">(large) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text size="3">(medium) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text size="2">(small) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text size="1">(tiny) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
</Canvas>

<!-- prettier-ignore-end -->

> The default `Text` variant is `medium`.

### Alternative Text

When passing the `alt` prop to the `Text` component, the alternative font will
be used.

<!-- prettier-ignore-start -->

<Canvas isColumn>
  <Text alt size="9">(huge) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text alt size="8">(h1) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text alt size="7">(h2) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text alt size="6">(h3) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text alt size="5">(h4) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text alt size="4">(large) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>

  <Text alt size="1">(tiny) The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>

<!-- prettier-ignore-end -->

### Text Color

Just as with the variants; while you can virtually attach any color from the
Theme to a `Text` component via the `css` prop, we took all the colors that
start with `$text-` and added a shorthand prop for calling them. For instance,
if there is a color named `$text-contrast`, the `color` prop you need to pass to
the `Text` component is `contrast`.

<!-- prettier-ignore-start -->

<Canvas isColumn>
  <Text color="default">The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text color="alt">The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text color="muted">The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text color="opposite" css={{backgroundColor: '$background-opposite'}}>The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
  <Text color="contrast">The Jedi Master trained the young Padawan</Text>
</Canvas>

<!-- prettier-ignore-end -->

> The default `Text` color is `default`
